Pyrostasis Resurrection – Part VI

Wheel of Misfortune

Two body scrapers were walking through a corridor where a fight occurred a few hours ago.

"Hideo, start writing!!!"

"Whatever you say, Ishikawa."

"Entry wound of first body, in upper left torso, pretty much at the heart, ejection wound at where the victim's spinal cord used to be. Time of death 14:04, according to nano machines timestamp in the black box. Ammunition used 7.62 mm parabellum round. Take a DNA sample and incinerate the corpse."

"What about the female?"

"Yes, entry wound would be left side of the skull and upper body torso. Ammunition fragments of a fragmentation grenade. Face is scarred beyond recognition. Time of death 14:05. What do you suggest we do, Hideo?"

"Dental and DNA samples to be on the safe side, then incinerate the corpse, Ishikawa."

"Like a magic missile, straight to the target, what does it show you?"

"That even after a millennia and a half, D&D is still alive and kicking, just with thousands of variations."

"Yeah, like all the classics such as Mage: The Ascension, Vampire: The Masquerade and the likes of them."

"I liked best the notion of Wraith: The Oblivion, where you start the game as a ghost and works to gain leverage in the under world." Hideo explained.

"But what about the crazy death squads in real life, which emerged after the 2787 A.D. supernova?"

"Unless they belong to Kalistor clan of Hell believers, they're simply hell believers, rather than dangerous terrorists..." Hideo mentioned but kept that line of thought: "How did Kalistor managed to sweep to his ranks so much power in the first place?"

"To show how dedicated he is to his cause, he chopped off both his arms with giant war axes, without anesthetics, live in front of millions."

"He flinched in the end, didn't he?"

"It's debatable, watch the clip in Shoutcast V.O.D, once we return to internet reception range."

"Why don't they commit suicide on mass proportions?"

"Because he trains them not to die meaningless deaths but glorious ones."

"Why? What difference does it make?"

"Because they don't want to be rookies at the dark lord army, but warlords."

"So he's the puppet master of them all..."

"More or less." Ishikawa stated.

"But how can he control so much in the hive, if rumour claims he never really was an inhabitant of the hive himself?"

"Now that's a whole different story..."
